1. Purpose

This document defines the business requirements for replacing the organisation's legacy Microsoft Access database used to manage plant variety intellectual property (IP): cultivar registration, licensing, royalty collection, block management, and cross-border movement of plant material. It forms the basis for the target data architecture and the phased migration plan.

2. Business objectives

  • BO-1 — Establish a single source of truth for licensing and royalty data to eliminate reconciliation errors.
  • BO-2 — Reduce the time to close a royalty collection cycle from weeks to days.
  • BO-3 — Provide a full audit trail from plant importation through to commercial sale.
  • BO-4 — Support concurrent multi-user access without file-locking conflicts.

5. Assumptions & constraints

  • Migration will be phased by subject area, starting with licensing and royalty (highest business risk per the systems audit).
  • Historical Access data will be cleansed and validated before migration; the legacy system remains the source of truth until cutover.
  • Target platform: PostgreSQL, to support concurrent access and enforced referential integrity (see data model).

6. Out of scope

Field-level apparatus systems (temperature and irrigation control) are excluded from this phase, consistent with the original systems audit scope.
